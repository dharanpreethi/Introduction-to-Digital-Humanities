Welcome to our Digital Humanities Lab

Digital Humanities is the bridge between traditional humanities and the ever-evolving digital landscape. It highlights both the opportunities and challenges of how digital tools can be applied to study and interpret human experiences. Combining computing technologies with traditional humanities disciplines like history, literature, philosophy, and cultural studies enables scholars to work together on projects that span continents and cultures. Digital tools facilitate the collection, analysis, and sharing of data in ways that were previously unimaginable, opening up new possibilities for collaboration across disciplines and regions, thus uncovering new insights into human behavior, culture, and society.

As an umbrella term, it includes all digital technologies that shape and are shaped by human experiences. From social media algorithms to digital archives, technology influences how we communicate, learn, and share knowledge. On the other hand, human creativity and behavior drive the evolution of these digital tools. The relationship is collaborative—each side continuously reshaping and refining the other. Digital Humanities explores and understands this exchange between tech and human experience.

At our Digital Humanities Lab, faculty, PhD scholars, and MA students collaboratively engage in innovative projects that push the boundaries of both digital and humanities research.

Our M.A. in Digital Humanities and Social Sciences program, offered through the Department of Humanities and Social Sciences at the Indian Institute of Technology Dhanbad, provides a comprehensive curriculum designed to prepare students for the dynamic intersection of technology and culture. Key courses include:

  • Introduction to Digital Humanities (IDH): An overview of the core concepts, tools, and methodologies of Digital Humanities.
  • Statistics: Foundational principles in statistical analysis relevant to humanities research.
  • Text Mining: Techniques for extracting meaningful patterns and trends from large corpora of texts.
  • Corpus Linguistics: The study of language patterns through large, structured sets of texts.
  • Natural Language Processing (NLP): Computational techniques for analyzing and understanding human language.
  • Digital Humanities Projects: Practical, project-based learning experiences that apply digital tools to humanities research.
  • Digital Ethics: Critical engagement with the ethical implications of digital technologies and data use.
  • Digital Society: Exploration of how digital transformations are reshaping social structures, behaviors, and cultural practices.
  • Digital Transformations: The study of how digital technology impacts human societies, culture, and economies.

Our program is designed to equip students with the technical skills and critical thinking necessary to address the complex challenges posed by the digital world while fostering interdisciplinary collaboration and innovative research.
